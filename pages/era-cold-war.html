❄️ 1945 — 1991

Cold War Proxy Wars

Two superpowers. Zero direct fights. Infinite countries destroyed in the crossfire.

The Cold War was the strangest conflict in history: a war between the United States and Soviet Union in which both sides were too afraid of nuclear weapons to actually fight each other. Instead, they fought everywhere else.

Korea. Vietnam. Cuba. Angola. Afghanistan. Nicaragua. For forty-five years, the two superpowers funded, armed, trained, and encouraged smaller nations to destroy each other on their behalf. The logic was that fighting in someone else's country was safer than fighting in your own. The people living in those countries had mixed feelings about this arrangement.

This section covers nine of the Cold War's major conflicts — from the frozen mountains of Korea to the jungles of Vietnam, from Cuba's sugar fields to Afghanistan's endless valleys. Each is a story of ideology meeting reality, and reality winning in messy, expensive, and entirely predictable ways.
